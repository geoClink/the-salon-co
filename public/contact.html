·Your First Visit

Four small things that’ll
make
the hour a better one.

I.

Arrive ten minutes early.

New guests are booked for an extended consultation. Tea or coffee is on the counter.

II.

Come with photos, if you like.

Or come with nothing at all. We’ll work from your hair, your life, and a long conversation.

III.

Skip the wash if you need to.

We can cut on day-old hair. We can’t cut what’s already wet from the rain.

IV.

Tell us about your week.

How you actually wear your hair on a Tuesday matters more than a Sunday photograph.

·Questions

The asked & the
answered.

The six we hear most often. If yours isn’t here, drop us a note — we’ll write back, and add it if it could be helpful to others.

info@thesalonco.com →
Do you take walk-ins? +

We don’t. Every appointment is booked one-per-hour, and the room is held for that guest. We do keep a short waitlist for next-day openings. Call the studio in the morning to be added.

How far out do you book? +

Most stylists are booked four to six weeks out for cuts, and six to eight weeks for color. Bridal and editorial work is booked eight to twelve weeks in advance.

Can I request a specific stylist? +

Yes. When booking, simply note who you’d like to see. If they’re not available in your window, we’ll suggest the next opening or offer someone whose work is similar.

What’s your cancellation policy? +

We ask for 48 hours notice. Cancellations inside that window are charged 50% of the service. We understand things happen — call us and we’ll work it out.

Do you offer gift cards? +

We do. Gift cards are available in any amount and can be purchased by calling the studio or emailing us. We’ll send a printed card or a digital version — your choice.

Is the studio accessible? +

We’re on the second floor with no lift, which we know isn’t ideal. If accessibility is a concern, call us — we’ll arrange an alternative and make it work.
